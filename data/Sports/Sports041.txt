2007 Cricket World Cup: Pakistan vs Ireland

Saturday, March 17, 2007Â 
Ireland stunned Pakistan by beating them by 3 wickets (with 32 balls remaining) in Group D at the World Cup at Sabina Park, Kingston, Jamaica.
After rain and bad light delayed proceedings, Ireland's target was reduced by the Duckworth-Lewis method to 128 runs from 47 overs.
Ireland's eventual victory eliminates Pakistan from the World Cup.
This was Ireland's first ever win over a team with test match status, and their first win in a World Cup (this tournament being their first appearance).
Toss: Ireland won, and elected to field first.
